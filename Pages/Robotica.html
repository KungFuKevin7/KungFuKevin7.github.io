Assignment Info

This was our final assignment of my second year in college. And it was by far the most fun i've ever had with a project. In this assignment we ICT-students teamed up with four students of mechanical engineering and two students of electrical engineering, to create a remote-controlled robot. In order to complete this assignment, our robot had to participate in a qualifying session and pass four tests. Those were:

  • Driving through an obstacle parcours with different types of terrain (i.e. grass, gravel, sand).
  • Drive the robot through a gate with a height of just 20cm.
  • Using a camera to perform an action everytime the camera detects a blue block.
  • Grab a cylinder of around one kilo, and weigh the exact weight of the cylinder (you were allowed to deviate five grams of the exact weight).
If you are interested, we have a marketing website also containing a blog of the entire building process. Click here to visit our website.

It was a very intensive project, but the coziness between all of us is what made this project so great. If i could do this project one more time, then sign me up!


My tasks

  • My first task was to get the servomotors (AX-12A) to work. These motors would function as the legs, this enabled the robot to stand and lay down on the ground.
  • My second responsibility was to create a TCP connection between a website and our robot, with this we were able to display realtime data on our website.
  • My third task was to get the DC-motors to work. These motors would power the wheels, which ultimately made the robot able to drive around.
  • My fourth task was to convert messages sent from the controller over bluetooth, to actual robot movement.
  • My final task was to create system that would allow the robot to communicate with real people. With this system you could ask questions to the robot and the robot would give an answer using text-to-speech.

Apart from the robot functionality, i was also responsible in designing the software using UML (4 + 1 Model).


What have i learnt?

I have learnt to work together with other people who specialize in other studies. I have also learnt to work under pressure, with a thight timing schedule. Programming-wise, i learnt how to write efficient code for embedded systems. I also learnt more advanced techniques and commands in the Linux terminal. I also taught myself more about TCP-sockets, how to establish a communication between two devices and sending messages between the two parties. Furthermore, i learnt how the bluetooth protocol works, and how you can translate that into robot movement. Lastly, i learnt how to work with OpenCV and how you can make a computer percieve objects in the real-world.
