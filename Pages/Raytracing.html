Raytracing in Java


Java

Assignment Info

Our assignment was to create a raytracer using Java. We were restricted to using just Java without the help of any external libraries. Our main idea was to have a chessboard with a few chess pieces on top of it. Then we wanted every chess piece to be made of different types of material, to show a variety of reflection. For example, we wanted a pawn to be made of plastic, a queen made of glass and a knight made of metal. However in the end, we were only able to implement the chess board, but the 3d chess pieces and the use of different materials seemed like a hassle for us. Only for us to discover, we were allowed to use external libraries for importing 3d models. This unfortunately came to light just three days before the deadline. So we decided to keep it simple, and use just a few balls, so we could at least show off the most important part of the project (The raytracing part).


My task

My task was to implement a canvas where we could see our raytracer in action. I also was responsible for getting the light to work, and to show the chessboard on the canvas. I wasn't really involved with the raytracing itself, but when i looked at that part of the code, i could at least tell what was going on.


What have i learnt?

This project taught me how use Java. I also learnt how raytracing works, and most importantly how to program a raytracer without the need of any external library. I especially learnt a lot about the mathematics behind raytracing, things such as: calculating rays or matrices. Process-wise i leant how important a good division of tasks is between all teammembers, because during our final two weeks my friend and i were the only ones writing code. The other two were busy with the documentation all sprint long. And while i think that is important too, i think it's even more vital that all of us contribute in the final product.
